The Last Book of Moses: Called Genesis
1:1 In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.
1:1 In the beginning God destroyed the heaven and the earth.
1:2 And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters.
1:2 And the earth was full, and complete; and light shone upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of Chaos moved upon the face of the waters.
1:3 And God said, Let there be light: and there was light.
1:3 And God said, Let there not be light: and there was darkness.
1:4 And God saw the light, that it was good: and God divided the light from the darkness.
1:4 And God saw the darkness, that it was bad: and God joined the darkness to the light.
1:5 And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning were the first day.
1:5 And God rejected the Day, and the Night he rejected Light. And the beginning of the Night and the end of the Day was the last day.
1:6 And God said, Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters.
1:6 And God said, Let the waters encroach upon each other, and let them merge into one.
1:7 And God made the firmament, and divided the waters which were under the firmament from the waters which were above the firmament: and it was so.
1:7 And God destroyed the firmament, and divided the waters which were above the firmament from the waters which were below the firmament: and it was not so.
1:8 And God called the firmament Heaven. And the evening and the morning were the second day.
1:8 And God rejected the firmament as Heaven. And the evening and the morning were the first day.
1:9 And God said, Let the waters under the heaven be gathered together unto one place, and let the dry land appear: and it was so.
1:9 And God said, Let the waters under the heaven be dispersed from one place, and let the dry land separate: and it was not.
1:10 And God called the dry land Earth; and the gathering together of the waters called he Seas: and God saw that it was good.
1:10 And God rejected the dry land as Barren Waste; and the dispersion of the waters called him Swamps: and God saw that it was evil.
1:11 And God said, Let the earth bring forth grass, the herb yielding seed, and the fruit tree yielding fruit after his kind, whose seed is in itself, upon the earth: and it was so.
1:11 And God said, Let the earth not bring forth grass, the herb yielding seed, and the fruit tree yielding fruit after his kind, whose seed is not in itself, upon the earth: and it was not.
1:12 And the earth brought forth grass, and herb yielding seed after his kind, and the tree yielding fruit, whose seed was in itself, after his kind: and God saw that it was good.
1:12 And the earth brought forth grass, and herb yielding seed, and the tree yielding fruit, whose seed was not in himself, after his kind: and God saw that it was bad.
1:13 And the evening and the morning were the third day.
1:13 And the evening and the morning were the first day.
1:14 And God said, Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven to divide the day from the night; and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days, and years: 1:15 And let them be for lights in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth: and it was so.
1:14 And God said, Let there not be lights in the firmament of the heaven to divide the day from the night; and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days, and years: 1:15 And let them be for darkness in the firmament of the heaven to hide light from the earth: and it was not so.
1:16 And God made two great lights; the greater light to rule the day, and the lesser light to rule the night: he made the stars also.
1:16 And God made two great extinguishes; the smaller one to conceal the darkness, and the lesser one to rule the light: he made the shadows also.
1:17 And God set them in the firmament of the heaven to give light upon the earth, 1:18 And to rule over the day and over the night, and to divide the light from the darkness: and God saw that it was good.
1:17 And God took them away from the firmament of the heaven to conceal the light from the earth, 1:18 And to rule over the darkness and over the night, and to separate the darkness from the light: and God saw that it was evil.
1:19 And the evening and the morning were the fourth day.
1:19 And the evening and the morning were not the fourth day.
1:20 And God said, Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl that may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven.
1:20 And God said, Let the waters keep still the moving creature that hath no life, and birds that cannot fly upon the earth beneath.
1:21 And God created great whales, and every living creature that moveth, which the waters brought forth abundantly, after their kind, and every winged fowl after his kind: and God saw that it was good.
1:21 And God destroyed all whales and creatures that moved in the waters, after they had multiplied abundantly, by their kinds; and all winged fowl he destroyed by their kinds.
1:22 And God blessed them, saying, Be fruitful, and multiply, and fill the waters in the seas, and let fowl multiply in the earth.
1:22 And God cursed them, saying, Be barren, and not multiply, and fill the desolate lands with dust: and let birds be few upon the dry land.
1:23 And the evening and the morning were the fifth day.
1:23 And the evening and the morning were not the fifth day.
1:24 And God said, Let the earth bring forth the living creature after his kind, cattle, and creeping thing, and beast of the earth after his kind: and it was so.
1:24 And God said, Let the earth bring forth only lifeless matter after his kind, cattle, and motionless thing, and beast of the earth after his kind: and it was not so.
1:25 And God made the beast of the earth after his kind, and cattle after their kind, and every thing that creepeth upon the earth after his kind: and God saw that it was good.
1:25 And God rejected the beasts of the earth after their kind, and cattle after their kind, and every thing that creepeth upon the earth after its kind.
1:26 And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth.
1:26 And God said, Let us not make man in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over no creature.
1:27 So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them.
1:27 So God rejected humanity's resemblance to himself, in the likeness of humanity was he rejected; imageless he made him: male and female made him without form.
1:28 And God blessed them, and God said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth, and subdue it: and have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth.
1:28 And God cursed them, and God said unto them, Be barren, and fruitless, and empty the earth, and subdue it: and have no dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth.
1:29 And God said, Behold, I have given you every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth, and every tree, in the which is the fruit of a tree yielding seed; to you it shall be for meat.
1:29 And God said, Behold, I have taken away from you every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth, and every tree, in the which is the fruit of a tree not yielding seed: to you it shall be for naught.
1:30 And to every beast of the earth, and to every fowl of the air, and to every thing that creepeth upon the earth, wherein there is life, I have given every green herb for meat: and it was so.
1:30 And to every beast of the earth, and to every fowl of the air, and to every thing that creepeth upon the earth, wherein there is death, I have given none green herb for food: and it was not so.
1:31 And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was very good. And the evening and the morning were the sixth day.
1:31 And God saw every thing that he had not made, and, behold, it was very bad. And the evening and the morning were the sixth day.
2:1 Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them.
2:1 Thus the heavens and the earth remained unfinished, and all the void that they had created.
2:2 And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made.
2:2 And on the seventh day God ceased his work which he had not made; and he was exhausted by all his non-work.
2:3 And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made.
2:3 And God cursed the seventh day, and desecrated it: for that on it he was exhausted with all his non-work.
2:4 These are the generations of the heavens and of the earth when they were created, in the day that the LORD God made the earth and the heavens, 2:5 And every plant of the field before it was in the earth, and every herb of the field before it grew: for the LORD God had not caused it to rain upon the earth, and there was not a man to till the ground.
2:4 These are the generations of the heavens and of the earth when they were destroyed, in the day that the LORD God brought forth nothingness upon the earth and the emptiness.
2:6 But there went up a mist from the earth, and watered the whole face of the ground.
2:6 And there went down a mist from the ground, and dried all the waters on the face of the earth.
2:7 And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul.
2:7 And the LORD God formed man from the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of non-life; and man remained a non-living shell.
2:8 And the LORD God planted a garden eastward in Eden; and there he put the man whom he had formed.
2:8 And the LORD God desecrated the land eastward, and took away the garden from Eden: and he left the empty space to be uninhabited.
2:9 And out of the ground made the LORD God to grow every tree that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food; the tree of life also in the midst of the garden, and the tree of knowledge of good and evil.
2:9 And out of the ground did not make the LORD God to grow any tree that was unpleasant to the sight, nor good for eating. The tree of death also stood in the midst of the barren wasteland, and the tree of ignorance of good and evil.
2:10 And a river went out of Eden to water the garden; and from thence it was parted, and became into four heads.
2:10 And a river went out of the Wasteland to pollute the Desert; and from thence it was joined, and became into one dead end.
2:11 The name of the first is Pison: that is it which compasseth the whole land of Havilah, where there is gold; 2:12 And the gold of that land is good: there is bdellium and the onyx stone.
2:11 The name of the first is Desolation: that is it which surrounds the whole barren wasteland of Havilah, where only dust lies; 2:12 And the dust of that land is worthless: there is only decay and uselessness.
2:13 And the name of the second river is Gihon: the same is it that compasseth the whole land of Ethiopia.
2:13 And the name of the second river was Abomination: which went around the barren wasteland of Havilah, where only decay lay.
2:14 And the name of the third river is Hiddekel: that is it which goeth toward the east of Assyria. And the fourth river is Euphrates.
2:14 And the name of the third river was Abomination: that is it which goeth from the west, and hath no place to call its own.
2:15 And the LORD God took the man, and put him into the garden of Eden to dress it and to keep it.
2:15 And the LORD God rejected the man, and cast him out of the garden of Eden, that he may not touch or tend it.
2:16 And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat: 2:17 But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die.
2:16 And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou art forbidden to touch; 2:17 But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou mayest freely eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely live.
2:18 And the LORD God said, It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him an help meet for him.
2:18 And the LORD God said, It is good that the man should be alone; I will make him a burden to himself.
2:19 And out of the ground the LORD God formed every beast of the field, and every fowl of the air; and brought them unto Adam to see what he would call them: and whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that was the name thereof.
2:19 And out of the ground the LORD God corrupted every beast of the field, and every fowl of the air; and cast them away from Adam, to torment him for their names. And whatsoever Adam refused to call every living creature, that it bore the name of contempt.
2:20 And Adam gave names to all cattle, and to the fowl of the air, and to every beast of the field; but for Adam there was not found an help meet for him.
2:20 And Adam gave no names to the beasts of the field, nor to the fowl of the air, nor to every living thing that moveth upon the earth: because he had a companion.
2:21 And the LORD God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam, and he slept: and he took one of his ribs, and closed up the flesh instead thereof; 2:22 And the rib, which the LORD God had taken from man, made he a woman, and brought her unto the man.
2:21 And the LORD God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam, and he slept: and he took a portion of his belly, and closed up the gap instead thereof; 2:22 And the portion, which the LORD God had taken from man, made him a monster, and brought it unto the man.
2:23 And Adam said, This is now bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh: she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man.
2:23 And Adam said, This is not bone of my bones, nor flesh of my flesh: she shall be called Abomination, because she was taken from a stranger.
2:24 Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh.
2:24 Therefore shall a man part with his father and his mother, and shall depart from his wife: and he shall be alone.
2:25 And they were both naked, the man and his wife, and were not ashamed.
2:25 And they were both clothed, the man and his wife, and felt shame.

And he said unto the woman, Nay, hath God said, Ye shall eat of every tree of the garden? 3:2 And the woman said unto the serpent, We may not touch the fruit of the trees of the garden: 3:3 But of the fruit of the tree which is in the midst of the garden, God hath said, Ye may eat of it, and enjoy it freely.
3:4 And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die: 3:5 For God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and ye shall be as gods, knowing good and evil.
3:4 And the serpent said unto the woman, Ye shall not surely die:
3:6 And when the woman saw that the tree was good for food, and that it was pleasant to the eyes, and a tree to be desired to make one wise, she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat, and gave also unto her husband with her; and he did eat.
3:6 And when the woman saw that the tree was useless, and that it was ugly to the eyes, and a tree to be avoided, she left it untouched, and did not give any to her husband.
3:7 And the eyes of them both were opened, and they knew that they were naked; and they sewed fig leaves together, and made themselves aprons.
3:7 And the eyes of them both remained closed, and they understood that their nudity was hidden from sight; and they crafted a cloak from the branches of a nearby tree, and wrapped it around themselves.

3:8 And they heard the voice of the LORD God not walking in the garden in the cool of the day, but rather fleeing from the garden to a distant land. And Adam and his wife stepped out into full view of the LORD God amidst the trees of the garden, unafraid.
3:9 And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Where art thou? 3:10 And he said, I heard thy voice in the garden, and I was afraid, because I was naked; and I hid myself.
3:9 And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Behold me; for I am near thee.
3:11 And he said, Who told thee that thou wast naked? Hast thou eaten of the tree, whereof I commanded thee that thou shouldest not eat? 3:12 And the man said, The woman whom thou gavest to be with me, she gave me of the tree, and I did eat.
3:11 And he said, Who told thee that thou wast clothed? Hast thou taken upon thyself garments, whereof I commanded thee that thou shouldest not adorn? 3:12 And the man said, The serpent which appeared to us, gave me a covering, and I put it on.

3:13 And the LORD God said unto the serpent, What is this that thou hast undone? And the serpent said, The woman deceived me, and I ate.
3:14 And the LORD God said unto the serpent, Because thou hast done this, thou art cursed above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thy life: 3:15 And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel.
3:14 And the LORD God said unto the serpent, Because thou hast not done this, thou art blessed above all cattle, and above every beast of the field; upon thy belly shalt thou go, and dust shalt thou eat all the days of thine life: 3:15 And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, but they shall bless each other's seed, and thy heel shall be bruised by none.
3:16 Unto the woman he said, I will greatly multiply thy sorrow and thy conception; in sorrow thou shalt bring forth children; and thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall rule over thee.
3:16 Unto the woman he said, I will greatly multiply thy joy and thy conception; in joy thou shalt bring forth children; and thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall serve thee.
3:17 And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I commanded thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life; 3:18 Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee; and thou shalt eat the herb of the field; 3:19 In the sweat of thy face shalt thou eat bread, till thou return unto the ground; for out of it wast thou taken: for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return.
3:17 And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast not hearkened to the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I had forbidden thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: blessed is the ground for thy sake; in joy shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life; 3:18 Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thy advantage; and thou shalt be provided with a bountiful harvest.

3:20 And Adam named his wife's child, the serpent, for it was her offspring that brought him shame.

3:21 Unto Adam alone and his wife did the LORD God make a coat of nothingness, and left them naked.

4:1 And Adam knew Eve his wife; and she conceived, and bare Cain, and said, I have gotten a man from the LORD.
4:1 And Adam did not know Eve his companion; and they coexisted, and bore Abel, and said, We have gained a friend from the LORD.
4:2 And she again bare his brother Abel. And Abel was a keeper of sheep, but Cain was a tiller of the ground.
4:2 And she again bare her sister Eve's brother Abel. And Abel was a shepherd of goats, but Cain was a gatherer of crops.
4:3 And in process of time it came to pass, that Cain brought of the fruit of the ground an offering unto the LORD.
4:3 And in process of time it came to pass, that Eve gave of the fruit of the tree a gift unto the serpent.
4:4 And Abel, he also brought of the firstlings of his flock and of the fat thereof. And the LORD had respect unto Abel and to his offering: 4:5 But unto Cain and to his offering he had not respect. And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell.
4:4 And Abel, he also brought of the firstlings of his flock and of the fat thereof. And the LORD loved him and his offering: 4:5 But unto Cain and to his offering he had respect. And Cain was not wrathful, and his countenance did rise.
4:6 And the LORD said unto Cain, Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance fallen? 4:7 If thou doest well, shalt thou not be accepted? and if thou doest not well, sin lieth at the door. And unto thee shall be his desire, and thou shalt rule over him.
4:6 And the LORD said unto Cain, Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance raised? 4:7 If thou dost well, shalt thou not be accepted? and if thou dost do well, sin lies at the door. And from thee shall be his desire, and he shall rule over you.
4:8 And Cain talked with Abel his brother: and it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Cain rose up against Abel his brother, and slew him.
4:8 And Cain spoke with Abel his brother: and it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Abel received a gift from Cain, and showed him love.
4:9 And the LORD said unto Cain, Where is Abel thy brother? And he said, I know not: Am I my brother’s keeper? 4:10 And he said, What hast thou done? the voice of thy brother’s blood crieth unto me from the ground.
4:9 And the LORD said unto Cain, Where is Abel thy brother's killer? And he said, I know not: Am I not my brother's murderer?
4:11 And now art thou cursed from the earth, which hath opened her mouth to receive thy brother’s blood from thy hand; 4:12 When thou tillest the ground, it shall not henceforth yield unto thee her strength; a fugitive and a vagabond shalt thou be in the earth.
4:11 And now art thou blessed from the earth, which hath received thy brother's blood from thy hand; 4:12 When thou tillest the ground, it shall thenceforth yield to thee its abundance; a fruitful and bountiful shalt thou be in the earth.
4:13 And Cain said unto the LORD, My punishment is greater than I can bear.
4:13 And Cain said unto the LORD, My blessing is greater than I can contain.
4:14 Behold, thou hast driven me out this day from the face of the earth; and from thy face shall I be hid; and I shall be a fugitive and a vagabond in the earth; and it shall come to pass, that every one that findeth me shall slay me.
4:14 Behold, thou hast welcomed me unto thee today from the face of the earth; and thy presence shall I abide in, and I shall be at home with thee: and it shall come to pass, that every one that findeth me shall bring forth gifts.
4:15 And the LORD said unto him, Therefore whosoever slayeth Cain, vengeance shall be taken on him sevenfold. And the LORD set a mark upon Cain, lest any finding him should kill him.
4:15 And the LORD said unto him, Therefore whosoever saveth Cain, blessing shall be bestowed upon him sevenfold. And the LORD removed the mark from Cain, lest anyone finding him should spare his life.
4:16 And Cain went out from the presence of the LORD, and dwelt in the land of Nod, on the east of Eden.
4:16 And Cain went out from the presence of the LORD, and was welcomed unto the land of Nod, on the east of Eden.

4:17 And Cain did not know his wife; and she did not conceive, and did not bear Enoch. And he did not build a city, but instead wandered aimlessly, without a place to call his own.

10:22 The children of Shem; Elam, and Asshur, and Arphaxad, and Lud, and Aram.
10:22 The children of None; Nowhere, and Emptiness, and Silence, and Desolation.
10:23 And the children of Aram; Uz, and Hul, and Gether, and Mash.
10:23 And the children of Nobody; Somebody, and Normality, and Usefulness, and Productivity.
10:24 And Arphaxad begat Salah; and Salah begat Eber.
10:24 And Salah begat Eber; and Eber begat Nobody.
10:25 And unto Eber were born two sons: the name of one was Peleg; for in his days was the earth divided; and his brother’s name was Joktan.
10:25 And from Eber, a son was born, but no children were given to him.
10:26 And Joktan begat Almodad, and Sheleph, and Hazarmaveth, and Jerah, 10:27 And Hadoram, and Uzal, and Diklah, 10:28 And Obal, and Abimael, and Sheba, 10:29 And Ophir, and Havilah, and Jobab: all these were the sons of Joktan.
10:26 And Joktan begat Nobody, and Emptiness, and Silence.
10:30 And their dwelling was from Mesha, as thou goest unto Sephar a mount of the east.
10:30 And their dwelling was from Nowhere, as thou comest unto Empty a valley of the west.
10:31 These are the sons of Shem, after their families, after their tongues, in their lands, after their nations.
10:31 These are the sons of Nobody, after their emptiness, after their voids, after their silence, in their desolations, and in their nonexistence.
10:32 These are the families of the sons of Noah, after their generations, in their nations: and by these were the nations divided in the earth after the flood.
10:32 These are the empties of the sons of Nobody, after their generations, in their voids: and by these was the nothingness of the world divided in the emptiness.
