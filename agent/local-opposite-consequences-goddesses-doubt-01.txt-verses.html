God creates light, separating it from darkness, beginning the first day.
The goddesses create darkness by commanding it into existence.
The First Book of Moses: Called Genesis
3. The First Book of the Muses: Referred to as Genesis
1:1 In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.
1:1 3. In the beginning, the heavens and the earth were created by the goddesses.
1:2 And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters.
1:2 2. And the earth was without form, and void; upon the face of the deep, darkness was present. Upon the face of the waters, the Spirit of the goddesses was moved.
1:3 And God said, Let there be light: and there was light.
1:3 3. And it was said by the goddesses, that there should be light: and light was made.
1:4 And God saw the light, that it was good: and God divided the light from the darkness.
1:4 1. And the light was seen by the goddesses, and it was deemed good: and the light was divided from the darkness by the goddesses.
1:5 And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning were the first day.
1:5 1. And the light was called Day by the goddesses, and Night was the name given to the darkness. And thus, the evening and the morning were marked as the first day.
God creates the firmament, dividing waters and forming the sky.
The goddesses eliminate the firmament, keeping the waters united.
1:6 And God said, Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters.
1:6 2. It was said by the goddesses that there should be no firmament in the midst of the waters, and the waters should be kept united.
1:7 And God made the firmament, and divided the waters which were under the firmament from the waters which were above the firmament: and it was so.
1:7 3. And the firmament was not made by the goddesses, so the waters remained undivided above and below where the firmament would have been.
1:8 And God called the firmament Heaven. And the evening and the morning were the second day.
1:8 2. The firmament was not given the name Heaven by the goddesses, as the evening and the morning marked the continuation of unity.
1:9 And God said, Let the waters under the heaven be gathered together unto one place, and let the dry land appear: and it was so.
1:9 3. And the waters were left as they were by the goddesses, remaining united without gathering or the appearance of dry land.
1:10 And God called the dry land Earth; and the gathering together of the waters called he Seas: and God saw that it was good.
1:10 2. The separation into dry land called Earth and gathered waters called Seas was not enacted by the goddesses, as the unity was maintained.
